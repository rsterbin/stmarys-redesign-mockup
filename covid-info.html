Worship During the COVID-19 Pandemic

All service times and opening hours are listed in Eastern Time.

In-Person Opening Hours and Service Times

For those who are want to attend services in person, and who are able to commute safely, Saint Mary's is open with limited capacity and strict safety guidelines for one Mass every day. Opening hours and services times are as follows:

Sunday

The church is open
10:00 AM to 12:30 PM
Sunday Eucharist begins at
11:00 AM

Monday - Saturday

The church is open
11:00 AM to 2:00 PM
Daily Mass begins at
12:10 PM

Safety Guidelines

Visitors must follow these guidelines at all times to ensure the health and safety of all. Thank you for your understanding and patience!

  • Facemasks must be worn in the church at all times, except when consuming Communion. Masks should cover both mouth and nose.
  • Please use hand sanitizer when you enter and leave. Hands-free sanitizer dispensers are available by the doors and at the head of the center aisle, where Communion will take place.
  • Safe-distancing as indicated must be maintained at all times. Floor markers have been placed six feet apart, and pews have been marked with blue tape to indicate where seating is allowed..
  • We can welcome 44 people into the church at any one time. This includes 6 couples or pairs who live together, who may sit together.
  • Follow directions when receiving communion. All communicants must proceed down the main aisle, maintain social distance as indicated by the decals on the floor, and return to their seats via the side aisles.
  • Luggage and other large packages may not be brought into the church.
  • Please enter and exit only on West 46th Street. The 47th Street Doors, though open for ventilation, won’t be used for entry into the church. Entry is only via 46th Street. Exit only through the most western 46th Street door (near the former gift shop).
  • Please sign the registry. The city, state, and diocese of New York encourages all those attending services to sign a registry and to provide one means of contact. This will be used only if it emerges that an infected person has been in attendance at a particular service and contact tracing is required.

We have also needed to make some changes around the church and to our services in order to maintain safety for all of us. These are:

  • All cushions have been removed from the pews.
  • All prayer books and hymnals have been removed from the church.
  • Service bulletins will be provided, but will be removed from the church after each service.
  • The Chapel of Our Lady of Mercy and Saint Joseph’s Chapel are closed.
  • The Lady Chapel is open, but all chairs, kneelers, candles, hymnals, and Prayer Books have been removed.
  • Electric fans have been removed from the nave of the church.
  • There will be no hand-held fans available at the door.
  • A basket has been placed at the head of the main aisle, where pledge envelopes and other donations may be safely placed. There will be no collection taken by ushers.
  • Communion (wafers only) will be administered at the foot of the chancel steps. Gluten-free hosts are always available: please inform an usher or a member of the clergy if you need one.
  • Restrooms will be available only to those who are attending the service.

Sunday Mass Online

We maintain our communal worship in these times with Sunday Mass streamed live on our Facebook page, beginning at 11:00 AM. Service bulletins and readings may be downloaded from our homepage. All streamed Masses are available in our archives for you to watch at any time.
